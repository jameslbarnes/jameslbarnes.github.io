I am currently working on Lore, a decentralized social network for generative art and storytelling on the Polygon blockchain.

During the last election cycle, I led product and measurement at ACRONYM, where my team and I built some pretty amazing tools to effectively measure and optimize ads against Donald Trump.

Before ACRONYM, I spent six years at Facebook, where I was the first Product Manager supporting Civic Integrity in the 2018 midterms, did early work on misinformation detection systems, and spent a year building the future of online-to-offline advertising. Before that, I gained some notoriety as the "Facebook embed" in Donald Trump's 2016 campaign, proving that both things and people are capable of great change.

I currently live in the East Village of Manhattan, and spent the three years before in San Francisco (the last of which I was grateful to live with my little brother in an epic apartment in the Mission), with a small and delightful interlude with my family in Tennessee. Before SF, I spent a decade in Washington, DC, and grew up in Middle Tennessee, where my family still lives.

In my free time, I'm a voracious reader, n00b marathon runner (2 marathons in 2020!), photographer, solo world traveler, committed yogi, festival junkie, and novice Judoka. I'm also a novice student of Taoism, and focus most of my energy trying to embody my favorite passage from the Tao Te Ching to mixed success:

Do you have the patience to wait til your mud settles and the water is clear? Can you remain unmoving til the right action arises by itself?
